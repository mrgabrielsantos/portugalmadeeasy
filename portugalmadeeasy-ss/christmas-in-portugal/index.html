    Christmas Day in Portugal

    Christmas Day in Portugal is a time for relaxation and family. Most presents are already opened, but sometimes friends or family may visit, bringing more gifts to share.

    Morning Mass and Religious Observances

    Many families start their day by attending a morning mass, which is a significant part of the holiday. This is a time for reflection and celebration of the birth of Jesus.

    Christmas Lunch and Desserts

    Lunch typically begins between 1 and 2 PM. The main dish often features:

    Visiting Relatives and Friends

    The rest of the day is usually spent with family, enjoying each other’s company. Many people watch Christmas movies or take a nap. Friends and extended family often drop by to share desserts and gifts.

    Festive Foods and Sweets

    Bolo Rei and Other Christmas Cakes

    One of the most beloved treats during Christmas in Portugal is Bolo Rei, a festive cake filled with dried fruits and nuts.     Portuguese Christmas dinners are incomplete without a variety of sweets. Here are some favorites:
